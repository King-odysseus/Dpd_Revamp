Africa isn’t harnessing its human capital for Development.

According to the World Economic Forum's Human Capital Index despite Africa’s huge demographic dividend, it only captures 55% of its human capital potential. This is manifests as:

  • Skills Mismatch and Unemployment
  • Brain Drain and Talent Emigration
  • Missed Opportunities for Development

The real problem

We theorize that at the root of this Challenge are:

  • Misalignment between Talent Production in academic institutions and the real development problems facing the continent.
  • Poor signaling (Talent matching) between directorates of employment, National Job banks in Africa and Production centers.
  • Pre- and in-service Workforce Development at scale.

The opportunity

  • According to a modelling study conducted by DPD, across 10 countries in Africa with the highest number of Universities, Africa has over 250,000 lecturers. Many are only focused on teaching and research but neglect other mandates such as: Grant making and Consultancy.
  • According to the Africa Union, over 70,000 skilled professionals emigrate from Africa to other climes annually. This represents important talent that can be reverse engineered to support the continent.
  • As financing for the SDG’s become more uncertain, mission driven clients have become more focused on efficiency and cost containment. One way to do this is through Fractionalized work-i. e reducing work to the skills required to do the job.
  • Africa also accounts for 10% of the world’s freelancers and only 2% of the global freelancing market, and this is likely to increase with the rise of fractional work , internet penetration and international mobility of talent.
  • An opportunity thus exists to better organize and harness Africa’s rich but ubiquitous talent to tackle its biggest challenges especially in Development.

Our solution

Our online talent marketplace aims to bridge the Signaling gap by curating top-tier technical talent in Africa from academia, industry, and independent spaces globally. By connecting Africa’s technical talent to Mission driven organizations willing to outsource tasks, projects or even hire, we are:

  • Increasing access and visibility of Africa’s development workforce
  • Promoting income generation for intellectual work.
  • Building a robust data pipeline to inform education and career choices in Africa.
